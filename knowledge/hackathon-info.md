# Poly Prompt 2026 Context

## Event Basics
- Event: Poly Prompt 2026
- Dates: February 20-22
- Location: Engineering 4, Room 106
- Audience: student teams building AI projects for student/college-life use cases

## Project Requirements
- Project must clearly target student or college life.
- AI must be used meaningfully in the product.
- Teams should also use AI in the build process and be able to explain how.
- Teams should aim for a working demo by Sunday, not just slides.
- Teams should pick one guiding category: Studying, Career, or College Life.

## Team Roles
- Teams should have role ownership in three areas:
- Tech Lead: technical direction and execution.
- Product Lead: problem clarity and demo/story.
- Ethics Lead: risks, responsible use, and mitigations.

## Judging Criteria and Weights
- Product Thinking: 20%
- Technical Impressiveness: 10%
- Impact: 10%
- Use of AI to Build: 10%
- Ethics / Responsible Use: 10%

## Demo and Submission Rules
- Final submissions are due Sunday at 3:00pm.
- Final presentations begin Sunday at 4:00pm.
- Presentation target is 5 minutes.
- There is no open Q&A block; judges may ask one follow-up max.
- Live or pre-recorded demos are both acceptable.

## High-Level Weekend Schedule
- Friday: kickoff + team alignment + early build time.
- Saturday: workshops + primary build day.
- Sunday: final build polish, submission cutoff, demos, and awards.

## Logistics and Communication
- Discord is the source of truth for announcements and team communication.
- Food is provided throughout the weekend.
- Snacks are available throughout.
- Saturday dinner is not provided.
- Teams should test demos before Sunday.

## AI and Quality Norms
- Use AI extensively and responsibly.
- Verify AI output and do not rely on it blindly.
- Teams should be transparent about how they used AI.

## Escalation Guidance for Poly
- If any rule/timing/scoring detail is uncertain, escalate to the organizer.
- If a team asks for exceptions to rules or deadlines, escalate to the organizer.
- If there is conflict between team claims and posted rules, escalate to the organizer.
